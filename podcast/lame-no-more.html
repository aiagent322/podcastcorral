Lame No More

Category: Horse Health

A veterinary-focused podcast dedicated entirely to equine lameness — diagnosis, treatment, rehabilitation, and prevention. The host is a board-certified equine surgeon who uses each episode to break down specific lameness conditions in language horse owners can actually use. Topics have included navicular syndrome versus palmar foot pain distinction, fetlock OCD management in young horses, kissing spine diagnosis and surgical versus conservative treatment options, and the growing use of MRI in standing sedated horses for foot and pastern evaluation. The show covers performance horse lameness specifically, including the biomechanical demands placed on barrel horses at the turn, and repetitive strain injuries common in heading and heeling horses. Guest veterinarians bring perspectives on regenerative medicine, shockwave therapy, and PRP injection protocols. The podcast does not shy away from second opinions, treatment failures, or the honest prognosis conversation owners dread having with their vet.
